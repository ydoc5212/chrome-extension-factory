# PSA: MV3 transition resumes (Nov 2023) — Oliver Dunk's first major Group post as Chrome DevRel

## TL;DR

In November 2023, Oliver Dunk (newly identified as the Chrome Extensions DevRel contact) posted the PSA announcing MV3 transition resumption; community pushback noted many MV2 use cases were still unaddressed, establishing the tension that defines his Group role.

## Signal

This PSA (Nov 16, 2023) is historically significant as it marks Oliver Dunk's visible entry into the chromium-extensions Google Group as the primary DevRel contact. The post is brief — it points to the blog post at developer.chrome.com/blog/resuming-the-transition-to-mv3/ and thanks the community for patience.

The immediate community response from wOxxOm (a highly respected technical contributor) is blunt: "Lots of reported use cases are still unaddressed and judging by the rate of progress so far you won't be able to address them in time." wOxxOm notably softens a harsher first draft because, as they note, "I realized it's likely that you genuinely believe in what you wrote since you've just recently started to participate in extension community." This meta-comment reveals that Oliver Dunk's role in the Group was new as of late 2023 — previously Simeon Vincent held this position.

The thread documents the community's ongoing MV3 skepticism going into the 2024 enforcement push, and positions Oliver Dunk as the new interface between Google and the developer community.

## Key quotes

> "We've just published a blog post announcing that we are resuming the transition to Manifest V3. It took a bit longer than we originally expected when we paused the MV2 deprecation timeline beginning this year. We really wanted to make sure that we'd addressed all the feedback that we've received."
> — Oliver Dunk, Nov 16, 2023

> "Lots of reported use cases are still unaddressed and judging by the rate of progress so far you won't be able to address them in time."
> — wOxxOm, Nov 16, 2023
